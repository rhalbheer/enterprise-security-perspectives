## About This Section

The **Security at AI Speed** series is for organizations that must now govern, operate, and lead in an environment where attackers use AI to compress the time between access and impact.

Everything here builds on one paper: **Your Security Model Is Too Slow to Matter**. It delivers the leadership reality check the rest of the series assumes. **Start there.** The follow-up papers translate that argument into an operating model, an execution path, and per-role guidance.

## Core Themes

Every paper in this section is grounded in the same set of strategic themes:

- **Decision latency as primary risk** — why the speed of seeing, deciding, and acting now determines outcomes more than control coverage.
- **Pre-authored authority** — moving human authority forward in time so automated response can execute without waiting for approval.
- **Threat-led governance** — replacing periodic review with a continuous operating loop that converts threat intelligence into enforceable decisions.
- **AI-speed attacks** — how adversarial use of AI compresses reconnaissance, compromise, movement, and impact into a single operating flow.
- **Identity as the control plane** — why standing privilege is a defect, and why AI agents must be governed as digital actors with lifecycle and scope controls.
- **CISO as decision engineer** — the shift from running a security program to operating a decision system under time pressure.
