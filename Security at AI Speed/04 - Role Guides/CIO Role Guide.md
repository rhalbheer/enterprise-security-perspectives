# Security at AI Speed — A Note for the Chief Information Officer

## 1. Why This Matters to You

The estate you run is now the terrain on which AI-speed attacks are fought. Identity, endpoint, cloud, data, and applications no longer behave as separate domains under separate stewardship — to an attacker they are a single graph of trust relationships, and your operating model is what decides whether that graph collapses under pressure or holds. Continuity of business services, delivery velocity, supplier dependencies, and recovery commitments now sit downstream of how fast your organization can see, decide, and act. That makes cyber resilience a property of how IT operates, not a parallel program adjacent to it. If the operating model is slow, fragmented, or unclear about authority, no amount of control coverage compensates.

## 2. The Core Problem

Risk is no longer driven by what is missing from a control library. It is driven by decision latency — the time between detection, decision, authorization, and action. Periodic governance, quarterly reviews, and escalation chains were built for a slower world. They produce false confidence: the program looks mature in reports while the operating model is structurally behind the attacker. Authority that is not pre-committed is authority that is unavailable when minutes matter. Coverage that depends on slow human interpretation does not change attacker momentum; it merely documents that defenders were present.

## 3. What You Must Own

- **One operational truth across the estate.** Identity, endpoint, cloud, data, and application telemetry must reconcile into a single, relationship-aware live map (a graph) that the board, security leadership, operations, and external providers all work from. Fragmented views are an organizational defect, not a tooling problem — and the estate is yours.
- **Identity debt as the most dangerous form of enterprise debt.** Standing privilege, legacy authentication islands, unmanaged service accounts, and over-broad entitlements are stored attacker acceleration. They convert administrative convenience directly into breach speed, and they sit squarely on your estate to retire.
- **Crown jewels defined operationally, not symbolically.** Name the specific business services, data classes, and dependencies whose disruption would create intolerable loss. "Mission critical" applied to everything is a refusal to choose — and the attacker will choose for you.
- **Recovery proven under realistic conditions for the systems that matter.** Recovery objectives stated in documents but never tested against the actual failure modes you would face are aspiration, not capability.
- **Lifecycle discipline as the default for every actor on the estate** — humans, workloads, service principals, and AI agents alike. If it has agency, it has identity, scope, telemetry, and a revocation path — or it does not run.
- **Authority decided before the incident.** Which identities can be suspended, which devices isolated, which sessions revoked, which services degraded, which traffic cut. If those decisions are not made now, they will be made under pressure with incomplete evidence.

## 4. What Must Change Now

- **From periodic review to continuous decision.** Governance becomes an operating loop, not a quarterly artifact. Validated exposure and attack paths best on threat intelligence drive decisions in days, not cycles.
- **From escalation to pre-authorization.** Codify the containment actions that may execute without waiting for a meeting. Pre-authored action is the only honest answer to machine-speed compromise.
- **From siloed dashboards to one shared map.** Consolidate identity, endpoint, cloud, data, and application signal into a single operational view (a graph) — different zoom levels for board, security leadership, and operations, but one truth underneath.
- **From standing privilege as convenience to standing privilege as defect.** Phishing-resistant authentication, time-bound access, and just-in-time elevation become baseline architecture across human and non-human actors, not an advanced aspiration.
- **From documented recovery to tested recovery.** Exercise realistic recovery scenarios against named crown-jewel services on a fixed cadence. Treat untested objectives as unverified claims.

## 5. The One Question to Take to the Executive Team

> **If the attacker chose tomorrow, which of our crown-jewel services would actually survive — and who in this room has already authorized what we are willing to interrupt to save them?**
