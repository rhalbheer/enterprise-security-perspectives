# Enterprise Security Perspectives

> **Practical cybersecurity thinking for leaders, architects, and practitioners** — written by [Roger Halbheer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rhalbheer/), Chief Security Advisor.

This repository is a living library of enterprise security perspectives: long-form papers, role-tailored briefs, and practical frameworks that help organizations turn cybersecurity from a cost center into a **strategic business enabler**. All content is free to read, share, and use.

---

## Why This Matters

Cybersecurity is no longer a purely technical discipline. Attackers move from first foothold to business impact in **minutes**, identity has replaced the network as the primary perimeter, and AI is accelerating the arms race on both sides. Organizations that treat security as a compliance checkbox are exposed; those that treat it as a **continuous business capability** gain a competitive edge.

This repository distils hard-won experience and current research into actionable frameworks — written for busy executives, technical leaders, and practitioners who need to act, not just read.

### 🌍 [Sovereignty in Practice](Sovereignity%20in%20Practice/)

A CISO-facing framework for **European Digital Sovereignty**, mapping practical scenarios to the [EU Cloud Sovereignty Framework](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/cloud-sovereignty-framework) (CSF) and Microsoft-centric controls.

| Document | Description | Link |
|----------|-------------|------|
| **European Digital Sovereignty In Practice** | Taxonomy aligned to EU CSF sovereignty objectives (SOV-1..SOV-8) and assurance levels (SEAL-0..SEAL-4); six practical scenarios with countermeasures and tradeoff guidance | [Markdown](Sovereignity%20in%20Practice/European_Digital_Sovereignty_in_Practice.md) |

**Core themes covered:**
- Why "sovereignty" is not binary — and how to avoid "sovereignty theatre"
- Eight sovereignty objectives (Strategic, Legal, Data & AI, Operational, Supply Chain, Technology, Security & Compliance, Environmental)
- Graded assurance levels (SEAL-0 through SEAL-4)
- Practical scenario mapping with people/process/technology/legal countermeasures
- Risk-management approach to sovereignty assessments per service or workload
